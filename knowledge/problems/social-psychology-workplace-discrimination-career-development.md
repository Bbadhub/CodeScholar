# Problem: Workplace Discrimination and Career Development

This problem involves understanding how workplace discrimination affects employee performance and career advancement, particularly for marginalized groups. It seeks to identify the psychological factors that contribute to disparities in career development opportunities.

## You Have This Problem If

- You notice a lack of diversity in leadership positions.
- Employees report feeling undervalued or overlooked for promotions.
- There are noticeable performance discrepancies among different demographic groups.
- Feedback from employees indicates experiences of discrimination or bias.
- You are developing initiatives to promote equality and inclusivity in the workplace.

## Start Here

**The recommended first approach is the Moderated Serial Mediation Model, as it allows for a nuanced understanding of the interplay between discrimination and career outcomes, which is essential for developing effective interventions.**
