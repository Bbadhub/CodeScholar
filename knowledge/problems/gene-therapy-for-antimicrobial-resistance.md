# Problem: Gene Therapy for Antimicrobial Resistance

Gene therapy for antimicrobial resistance involves using genetic modification techniques to combat infections caused by antibiotic-resistant bacteria. This approach aims to provide alternative treatments and enhance the effectiveness of existing therapies against superbugs.

## You Have This Problem If

- You are facing persistent infections that do not respond to standard antibiotics.
- You are involved in research focused on genetic modification in microbiology.
- You are exploring innovative treatments for antibiotic-resistant pathogens.
- You have encountered a rise in superbugs in clinical settings.
- You are looking for alternative therapeutic strategies in infectious disease management.

## Approaches

**Best for:** Developing new gene therapies for antibiotic-resistant infections.

**Tradeoff:** While CRISPR offers precise editing capabilities, it requires careful design to avoid off-target effects.
