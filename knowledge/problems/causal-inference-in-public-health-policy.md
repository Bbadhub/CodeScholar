# Problem: Causal Inference in Public Health Policy

Causal inference in public health policy involves determining the effects of health interventions and policies on public health outcomes. This is crucial for designing effective health policies and evaluating their impact on populations.

## You Have This Problem If

- You are trying to evaluate the effectiveness of a health intervention.
- You need to understand the causal relationship between a policy and health outcomes.
- You are working with observational data in public health.
- You are designing a new health policy aimed at improving health metrics.
- You are conducting research that requires robust statistical analysis.
