## Method

**Approach:** The OEJSR method utilizes the Cuckoo Search Optimization algorithm to explore potential solutions while employing the Grey Wolf Optimization to refine these solutions. This hybrid approach aims to balance exploration and exploitation for optimal job scheduling in cloud environments.

**Algorithm:**

1. Initialize population of solutions (nests) using Cuckoo Search.
2. Evaluate the fitness of each solution based on resource allocation.
3. Apply Grey Wolf Optimization to enhance the best solutions.
4. Replace less fit solutions with new solutions generated by Cuckoo Search.
5. Iterate until convergence criteria are met.

**Input:** Job requirements, resource availability, and cloud environment parameters.

**Output:** Optimized job scheduling plan with allocated resources.

**Key Parameters:**

- `population_size: 50`
- `max_iterations: 100`
- `cuckoo_rate: 0.25`
- `alpha: 1.5`

**Complexity:** O(n log n) time, O(n) space

## Abstract

Cuckoo Search Optimization (CSO) is a metaheuristic algorithm inspired by the unique reproductive behavior of certain cuckoo bird species. These birds lay their eggs in the nests of other bird species, relying on the host birds to incubate and raise their offspring. If the host bird detects an alien egg, it may either abandon the nest or destroy the egg. This ensures that only the most deceptive and well-hidden eggs survive. This biological process serves as a metaphor in CSO, where "eggs" represent potential solutions to an optimization problem, and "nests" are different candidate solutions in the search space.
