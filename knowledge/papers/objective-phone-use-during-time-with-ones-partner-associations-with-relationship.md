## Summary

This paper investigates the impact of phone use on interpersonal relationships, specifically how it affects the quality of time spent with partners. Engineers should care because understanding these dynamics can inform the design of applications and tools aimed at enhancing relationship well-being.

## Key Contribution

**The study provides empirical evidence linking phone use during partner interactions to diminished relationship satisfaction and individual well-being.**

## Problem

The real-world problem motivating this work is the negative impact of smartphone usage on face-to-face interactions and relationship quality.

## Abstract

One person’s phone use can intrude on face-to-face time with their partner. It can subtly displace connection, attention, and responsiveness. The term is generally used pejoratively, to describe moments when someone feels sidelined because their partner is absorbed in a screen instead of the relationship that’s right in front of them.
