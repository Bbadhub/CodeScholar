## Summary

This paper explores the application strategies of brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) in educational settings through the lens of innovation diffusion theory. Engineers should care because it highlights how emerging technologies like BCIs can be integrated into educational practices, potentially transforming learning experiences.

## Problem

## Method

**Approach:** The method involves analyzing the factors influencing the adoption of BCIs in education through innovation diffusion theory. It identifies key strategies for integrating BCIs into educational practices and assesses their potential impact on learning.

**Algorithm:**

1. Identify key stakeholders in the educational sector.
2. Analyze the current state of technology adoption in education.
3. Evaluate the perceived benefits and challenges of BCIs.
4. Develop strategies for promoting BCI adoption based on innovation diffusion theory.
5. Implement pilot programs to test BCI applications in educational settings.
6. Gather feedback and assess the effectiveness of BCIs in enhancing learning.

**Input:** Data on current educational practices, stakeholder perceptions, and technological capabilities.

**Output:** A set of strategies and recommendations for BCI implementation in education.

**Key Parameters:**

- `stakeholder_engagement: high`
- `pilot_program_duration: 6 months`
