## Summary

This paper investigates the factors influencing the adoption of social chatbots among digital natives, particularly focusing on the motivations behind their usage. Engineers should care because understanding these factors can help in designing more engaging and effective chatbot systems tailored for younger users.

## Key Contribution

**Identifying key psychological and social factors that drive the adoption of social chatbots among Gen Z and Gen Alpha.**

## Problem

The increasing popularity of social chatbots among younger generations raises questions about the underlying motivations for their usage.

## Method

**Approach:** The method involves assessing various psychological and social factors that influence the usage of social chatbots. Surveys and interviews are conducted to gather data on user motivations, which are then analyzed to identify trends and insights.

**Algorithm:**

1. 1. Define the target demographic (e.g., Gen Z and Gen Alpha).
2. 2. Develop a survey instrument to assess motivations for using chatbots.
3. 3. Collect data from participants through surveys and interviews.
4. 4. Analyze the data to identify key factors influencing chatbot adoption.
5. 5. Present findings and recommendations for chatbot design.

**Input:** Survey responses and interview data from users.

**Output:** Insights into user motivations and recommendations for chatbot design.

**Key Parameters:**

- `sample_size: 100-500`
- `demographic_age_range: 10-25`
