## Summary

This study investigates the relationship between trait anger, revenge motivation, and cyberbullying perpetration among adolescents, highlighting the mediating role of revenge motivation and the moderating effect of online disinhibition. Engineers should care because understanding these dynamics can inform the development of targeted interventions to mitigate cyberbullying behaviors.

## Key Contribution

**The study identifies revenge motivation as a significant mediator between trait anger and cyberbullying perpetration, moderated by online disinhibition.**

## Problem

The research addresses the increasing prevalence of cyberbullying among adolescents, particularly in the context of Chinese cultural norms and online behavior.

## Method

**Approach:** The method employs a moderated mediation model to analyze the relationships between trait anger, revenge motivation, online disinhibition, and cyberbullying perpetration. It uses statistical techniques to assess direct and indirect effects.

**Algorithm:**

1. 1. Collect data from participants using online questionnaires.
2. 2. Measure trait anger, revenge motivation, online disinhibition, and cyberbullying perpetration using validated scales.
3. 3. Conduct correlation analysis to explore relationships between variables.
4. 4. Use Hayes's PROCESS macro for SPSS to test the moderated mediation model with bootstrapping.
5. 5. Analyze the results to determine the significance of mediation and moderation effects.

**Input:** Data from online questionnaires assessing trait anger, revenge motivation, online disinhibition, and cyberbullying perpetration.

**Output:** Statistical results indicating the strength and significance of relationships between variables.

**Key Parameters:**

- `Sample size: 1574`
- `Bootstrapping samples: 5000`
- `Significance level: p < 0.001`

**Complexity:** Not stated

## Benchmarks

**Tested on:** 1,574 Chinese adolescents' responses to online questionnaires

**Results:**

- Trait anger (mean: 1.70, SD: 0.50)
- Revenge motivation (mean: 2.16, SD: 1.07)
- Cyberbullying perpetration (mean: 1.05, SD: 0.20)

**Compared against:** Previous studies on trait anger and cyberbullying

**Improvement:** The study provides insights into the mediating role of revenge motivation and the moderating role of online disinhibition, which were not previously established.
