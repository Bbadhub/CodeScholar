## Summary

The study investigates the anterior midcingulate response to reward in opioid users and demonstrates that this response can be normalized through prefrontal transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS). Engineers should care because this approach could lead to new therapeutic interventions for addiction treatment.

## Key Contribution

**Normalization of the anterior midcingulate response in opioid users through TMS.**

## Problem

The work is motivated by the need to address altered reward processing in opioid users, which contributes to addiction.

## Method

**Approach:** The method involves applying TMS to the prefrontal cortex to stimulate neural activity, which in turn normalizes the reward response in the anterior midcingulate cortex of opioid users. This approach leverages the brain's plasticity to potentially restore normal function.

**Algorithm:**

1. 1. Identify opioid users with blunted anterior midcingulate response.
2. 2. Prepare TMS equipment and set parameters for stimulation.
3. 3. Apply TMS to the prefrontal cortex at specified intervals.
4. 4. Measure anterior midcingulate response to reward stimuli pre- and post-TMS.
5. 5. Analyze changes in neural response to determine normalization.

**Input:** Data on opioid users' brain activity and reward response metrics.

**Output:** Normalized anterior midcingulate response to reward in opioid users.
